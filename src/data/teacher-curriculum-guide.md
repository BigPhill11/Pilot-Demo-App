# Pilot: Teacher Curriculum Guide
### A Complete Overview of the Platform's Learning Experience

---

## What Is Pilot?

Pilot is a financial literacy and career readiness platform built around one core idea: **learning by doing**. Students don't just read about money — they simulate real financial decisions, explore actual career paths, and build the professional skills that matter in the real world. Every module connects classroom concepts to the choices students will face as young adults.

The platform is organized into four main learning areas:

1. **Personal Finance Education** — The foundation of the platform
2. **Market Intelligence** — The connective tissue between personal finance and the professional world
3. **Careers in Finance** — Deep dives into professional paths, including how AI is reshaping each one
4. **Soft Skills & Professional Development** — The human side of career success

# PART 1: PERSONAL FINANCE EDUCATION

> *The heart of Pilot. This is where students build the financial foundation that will define their adult lives.*

Personal Finance is the most expansive section of the platform. Nine structured modules take students from complete beginner to advanced financial thinker. What makes Pilot different is the **real-world simulation model**: students don't just learn concepts — they apply them in decision-making scenarios, Boss Challenge games, and simulated life situations before they ever face those choices in real life.

Each module follows a consistent structure:
- **5 core lessons** built around real scenarios and decision points
- **Mini-quizzes** after each lesson for immediate comprehension checks
- **A Boss Challenge game** at the end of each module — a gamified simulation that puts the whole module to the test
- **A test-out option** for students who already have mastery

## The Real-World Simulation Model

What sets Pilot apart is that every concept is tied to a decision. Students aren't asked to memorize definitions — they're put into scenarios:

- *You just got your first paycheck. Here's what's on it. What do you do?*
- *An emergency just cost you $3,000. Do you have an emergency fund? What happens if you don't?*
- *The market just dropped 20%. Do you sell, hold, or buy more?*
- *You have $500 extra this month. Pay down debt or invest?*

The **Boss Challenge games** at the end of each module are the simulation engine. They compress the entire module's lessons into one extended scenario where students have to make real choices with real consequences inside the game. The feedback isn't "wrong answer" — it's *here's what this decision would cost you over 10 years.*

# PART 2: MARKET INTELLIGENCE

> *The glue of the platform. Market Intelligence is where personal finance meets the professional financial world.*

If Personal Finance teaches students how to manage their own money, **Market Intelligence is where they start to understand how the economy and markets actually work** — and how to read them. This section bridges the gap between a student's personal financial decisions and the larger world of business, investing, and economics.

Market Intelligence is organized into four tracks, each building on what students learned in Personal Finance and previewing the professional world explored in Careers in Finance.

---

## Track 1: Business Economics
**Core Idea:** *Understanding how businesses, markets, and economies actually operate*

| Module | Topic |
|--------|-------|
| The Price of Bamboo | Supply, demand, and why prices move — connecting macro forces to personal finance |
| Who's the Competition? | Market structures, competitive moats, and how businesses protect their position |
| The Big Picture | Economic cycles, recessions, and how to recognize where the economy is in its cycle |
| Money's Temperature | Interest rates, inflation, and why they affect everything from your savings account to the stock market |

**Personal Finance Connection:** Students immediately see how economic cycles affect their own financial decisions — when to lock in a mortgage rate, why their savings account rate changes, why inflation erodes purchasing power.

**Career Connection:** This is the vocabulary of business. Investment bankers, consultants, corporate finance professionals, and wealth managers all use these concepts daily.

---

## Track 2: Markets & Headlines
**Core Idea:** *Learning to read financial news and understand what it actually means*

| Module | Topic |
|--------|-------|
| Headline Decoder | How to read financial news without being misled — distinguishing signal from noise |
| Noise vs. Signal | Identifying which news actually matters for investors versus clickbait |
| The Earnings Call | How quarterly earnings reports work and what they reveal about a business |
| Market Mood | Market sentiment, investor psychology, and how emotion drives price movements |

**Personal Finance Connection:** Students who understand market psychology become better long-term investors — they learn to recognize when fear and greed are driving markets rather than fundamentals.

**Career Connection:** This track introduces the analytical lens that buy-side and sell-side professionals use. Reading the tape, understanding earnings, and interpreting sentiment are core skills in investment management, trading, and financial journalism.

---

## Track 3: Ownership
**Core Idea:** *What it actually means to own a piece of a business*

| Module | Topic |
|--------|-------|
| Own a Piece | Stock ownership — what shareholders actually own and what rights come with it |
| Time Is Your Ally | Compound growth — the mathematics of patient investing |
| The Patience Game | Why long-term investors outperform traders and how to build the discipline to stay the course |
| Mistakes That Matter | The most common investing mistakes, what they cost, and how to avoid them |

**Personal Finance Connection:** This track is a direct extension of the Investing module in Personal Finance — students go deeper on *what* they're buying and *why* long-term ownership works.

**Entrepreneurship Element:** The concept of ownership extends beyond the stock market. Students begin to understand equity — whether in a company they invest in or a business they might build themselves.

---

## Track 4: Insider Knowledge
**Core Idea:** *Speaking the language of the financial world and understanding how insiders think*

| Module | Topic |
|--------|-------|
| The Network Effect | Business moats — what makes a company defensible and how professionals evaluate competitive advantage |
| Speaking the Language | Core financial terminology that professionals use every day — learning to decode earnings calls, filings, and pitchbooks |
| From Learner to Insider | Putting it all together — how to start thinking like a financial professional |

**Accounting Introduction:** This track introduces students to the language of accounting without making it a full accounting course. They learn to read a basic income statement, understand what revenue vs. profit means, and recognize why cash flow matters — giving them enough fluency to understand what professionals are talking about.

**Career Bridge:** Market Intelligence is explicitly designed to introduce students to the idea that there are *careers* built around understanding these concepts. By the end of this section, students are ready to explore the Careers in Finance section with real context.

---

## Why Market Intelligence Is the Glue

Market Intelligence occupies a unique position in Pilot:

- It **reinforces Personal Finance** by showing students how individual decisions (saving, investing, taking on debt) exist within a larger economic system
- It **previews Career possibilities** by exposing students to the frameworks and vocabulary that financial professionals use
- It introduces **business ownership and entrepreneurship** — the idea that building something is another form of financial power
- It provides **accounting literacy** at an accessible level — enough to read a company's health, not enough to scare anyone away
- It teaches students to **think critically about financial information** in a world saturated with it

# PART 3: CAREERS IN FINANCE

> *For students who want to go further — understanding what working in finance actually looks like.*

The Careers in Finance section is organized as a **Career Jungle** — four distinct ecosystems, each representing a different corner of the financial world. Within each ecosystem, students can explore specific career paths through multi-level journeys that show what the job actually involves day-to-day.

Every career path includes a **7-level progressive journey**, term glossaries, flashcard sets, and story-based scenarios that put students in the role before they've ever applied for it.

---

## The Four Career Ecosystems

### 1. The Trading Canopy — Sell Side
*Banks and institutions that create, structure, and sell financial products*

| Career Path | What They Do |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Investment Banking** | Help companies raise capital, execute mergers and acquisitions, and navigate major financial transactions — the most prestigious and demanding entry point in finance |
| **Sales & Trading** | Buy and sell securities on behalf of clients and the firm, reading markets in real time |
| **Commercial Banking** | Manage relationships with businesses, provide lending, and structure credit facilities |

**Investment Banking Deep Dive — 6 Sub-Divisions:**
Within Investment Banking, students can explore the specific divisions that make up a full-service bank:

| Division | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| M&A | Mergers and acquisitions — buying and selling companies |
| ECM (Equity Capital Markets) | Helping companies go public (IPOs) and raise equity |
| DCM (Debt Capital Markets) | Helping companies borrow at scale through bonds and credit |
| Leveraged Finance | Financing highly leveraged transactions like private equity buyouts |
| Restructuring | Helping financially distressed companies reorganize or recover |
| Secondaries | Facilitating the secondary sale of private market assets |

---

### 2. The Growth Grove — Buy Side
*Firms that invest capital on behalf of clients, endowments, or themselves*

| Career Path | What They Do |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Wealth Management** | Help individuals and families grow and protect their wealth — a client-relationship-driven career that blends personal finance with investing |
| **Asset Management** | Manage large pools of capital (mutual funds, ETFs, pension funds) on behalf of institutional and retail investors |
| **Hedge Funds** | Deploy sophisticated strategies — long/short, macro, quantitative — in pursuit of absolute returns |
| **Private Equity** | Acquire companies, improve them operationally, and sell them at a profit — one of the most coveted paths in finance |
| **Venture Capital** | Fund early-stage startups, bet on founders, and build the next generation of companies |

---

### 3. The Wisdom Clearing — Advisory
*Firms that provide strategic guidance to businesses*

| Career Path | What They Do |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Management Consulting** | Help organizations solve their hardest problems — strategy, operations, transformation — working across industries and functions |

**Management Consulting Journey (7 Levels):**
1. Introduction to Consulting — what consultants actually do
2. The Role of the Consultant — stakeholder management and problem framing
3. Consulting Frameworks — MECE, issue trees, 2x2s
4. Data & Analysis — turning information into insight
5. Strategy Recommendations — building the narrative and the slide
6. Implementation & Change Management — making recommendations stick
7. Trends & The Future — AI, sustainability, and where consulting is headed

---

### 4. The Bamboo Headquarters — Corporate Finance
*The financial engine inside every major company*

| Career Path | What They Do |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Corporate Finance** | Manage a company's financial strategy from the inside — FP&A, treasury, investor relations, capital allocation — the financial backbone of every business |

---

## How the Career Journeys Work

Each major career path features a **7-level progressive journey**:

- **Level 1** introduces the career — what it is, who does it, what a typical day looks like
- **Levels 2–5** go progressively deeper — the skills, the tools, the language, the deals
- **Level 6** covers advanced concepts and what separates good professionals from great ones
- **Level 7** zooms out — trends shaping the field, where the industry is going, and what it means for someone entering today

Students also have access to **career stories** — narrative accounts from people inside Investment Banking, Private Equity, and Consulting — showing the reality of the path, not just the prestige.

---

## AI & The Future of Finance Careers

A critical thread running through the Careers in Finance section is **how artificial intelligence is changing each profession**. This isn't presented as a threat — it's presented as the landscape every student entering finance will need to understand.

Within each career journey, students explore:

| Career | AI Impact |
|--------|-----------|
| **Investment Banking** | AI-assisted financial modeling, automated due diligence screening, LLMs drafting pitch materials — what analysts actually spend their time on is shifting |
| **Asset Management** | Quantitative and algorithmic strategies, AI-driven portfolio optimization, natural language processing for earnings call analysis |
| **Hedge Funds** | Machine learning for alpha generation, sentiment analysis, pattern recognition at scale — the line between quant and fundamental funds is blurring |
| **Private Equity** | AI-powered deal sourcing, automated portfolio monitoring, LLMs for market mapping and target identification |
| **Venture Capital** | AI for startup screening, predictive signals on founder and team quality, automated reference checking |
| **Wealth Management** | Robo-advisors and hybrid models, AI-driven financial planning tools, what human advisors must offer that algorithms can't replicate |
| **Management Consulting** | LLMs for research synthesis and slide drafting, automation of analytical tasks — what stays human is judgment, relationships, and implementation |
| **Corporate Finance** | AI for financial forecasting, anomaly detection, automated reporting — finance teams are getting smaller but more strategic |

The message to students: **AI fluency is no longer optional in finance. The professionals who understand both the financial fundamentals and how to work alongside these tools will have an enormous advantage.**

# PART 4: SOFT SKILLS & PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT

> *The human side of career success — because technical skills get you the interview, but soft skills get you the job and the promotion.*

The Soft Skills section is dedicated to the professional behaviors and communication skills that schools rarely teach but employers always evaluate. These aren't abstract concepts — they're practical, learnable skills that students can begin applying immediately.

---

## The Soft Skills Curriculum

### Professional Interviewing
**Core Question:** *How do you present your best self under pressure?*

Students learn the full interview lifecycle — from preparation to follow-up:
- Understanding what interviewers are actually evaluating
- Structuring answers using behavioral frameworks (STAR method)
- Researching companies and roles before you walk in
- Handling difficult questions, gaps, and pivots
- Following up effectively after the interview
- Understanding the difference between campus recruiting and professional interviews

---

### Networking
**Core Question:** *How do you build real relationships in a professional context?*

- The mindset shift: networking as giving, not asking
- How to reach out to professionals effectively
- Informational interviews — how to ask for them and what to do with them
- Working a room at professional events
- Building and maintaining a professional network over time
- Using LinkedIn strategically without being transactional

---

### Business Communication
**Core Question:** *How do you communicate like a professional in writing and in person?*

- Writing professional emails — structure, tone, subject lines, and response etiquette
- Meeting communication — how to prepare, contribute, and follow up
- Communicating with superiors vs. peers vs. direct reports
- How to give and receive feedback professionally
- Presentation skills — structuring a message, reading the room, handling questions

---

### Business Attire — Dress for Success
**Core Question:** *How does appearance communicate professionalism before you say a word?*

- Understanding dress codes: business formal, business professional, business casual, smart casual
- Building a professional wardrobe on a student budget
- Industry-specific norms — what's appropriate in banking vs. tech vs. consulting
- The psychology of professional appearance — how what you wear affects how you're perceived and how you perform
- Common mistakes and how to avoid them

---

### Workplace Etiquette
**Core Question:** *How do you navigate the unwritten rules of professional environments?*

- Punctuality, responsiveness, and reliability — the basics that many overlook
- Office culture and reading the room in a new workplace
- Managing up — how to work effectively with managers
- Handling conflict professionally
- Understanding boundaries and professional norms
- Virtual work etiquette for remote and hybrid environments

---

### Working Women Excellence
**Core Question:** *What does navigating professional environments look like for women specifically?*

A dedicated track addressing the unique dynamics women face in professional settings:
- Negotiating salary and advancement as a woman
- Navigating male-dominated industries
- Building allies and sponsors
- Managing visibility and voice in meetings
- The double bind and how to move through it strategically

---

### Black in Business Excellence
**Core Question:** *What does professional success look like for Black professionals in finance and business?*

A dedicated track honoring the specific experiences and strategies of Black professionals:
- Code-switching, authenticity, and professional identity
- Building community and finding mentors in predominantly white spaces
- Navigating bias in hiring and promotion
- Historical and contemporary context of Black wealth and business
- Career strategy for breaking into and advancing in finance

---

### Diversity & Inclusion
**Core Question:** *How do we build workplaces where everyone can do their best work?*

- Understanding unconscious bias and its impact
- Being an effective ally in professional settings
- Inclusive communication practices
- What DEI actually means in a corporate context — beyond the talking points

# SUMMARY FOR EDUCATORS

## How the Platform Connects

Pilot is designed as an **integrated learning ecosystem**, not a collection of independent modules. Here's how the four sections connect:

```
Personal Finance → builds the foundation
        ↓
Market Intelligence → adds economic context and bridges to the professional world
        ↓
Careers in Finance → shows where the knowledge leads professionally
        ↓
Soft Skills + Phil's Friends → prepares students for the human side of those careers
```

## Assessment & Progression

- Every module includes **quizzes after each lesson**
- Every Personal Finance module ends with a **Boss Challenge simulation game**
- Students can **test out** of modules they've already mastered
- The **adaptive flashcard system** reinforces retention across all three main content areas
- The **Bamboo Empire** gamification system rewards consistent engagement with XP, coins, and skill progression

## The Ask Phil Feature

Every student has access to **Ask Phil** — an AI-powered tutor built on the platform's full curriculum. Students can ask any financial question and receive a clear, contextual answer. Ask Phil can explain a concept from a lesson, help a student work through a simulation decision, or answer a question that came up from a Phil's Friends video. It's the always-available teacher.

---

## Designed for Today's Students

Pilot was built for the generation that will manage their finances in a more complex, technology-driven, and uncertain economic environment than any before them. The platform teaches:

- **Financial foundations** that protect them in adulthood
- **Economic and market literacy** that makes them informed citizens and better investors
- **Career knowledge** that opens doors they didn't know existed
- **Professional skills** that help them walk through those doors with confidence
- **Human connection** through Phil's Friends — proof that these paths are possible

**Pilot doesn't just teach students about money. It prepares them to build a life with it.**
